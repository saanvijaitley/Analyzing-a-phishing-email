Task 2: Phishing Email Analysis

Objective

Analyze a suspicious email to identify phishing indicators, such as spoofed addresses, fake links, urgent tone, and poor grammar.

Tools Used

  • Basic text editor (Notepad)
  • Email header analyzer (MXToolbox)
  • Web browser for link inspection

Phishing Indicators Found

  • Spoofed Sender: Email claimed to be from a bank, but sender domain was fake.
  • Suspicious Link: Displayed link looked safe, but real URL redirected to unknown site.
  • Urgent Language: Email warned of account deactivation if user didn't act fast.
  • Generic Greeting: "Dear Customer" instead of name.
  • Grammar Errors: Minor spelling and formatting mistakes.

Conclusion

This email exhibited multiple phishing signs. Users should avoid clicking suspicious links and verify with the organization directly. This task demonstrates how to critically evaluate suspicious emails.

## Sample email Subject: Urgent Action Required - Account Suspended Date: 12 May 2021 23:21:48 +0200 From: support@bank-verify.com(link sends e-mail)> To: recipient@berkeley.edu(link sends e-mail) Dear Customer, We have detected suspicious activity on your bank account. Your access has been temporarily suspended for security reasons. To restore access, please verify your account immediately: http://secure-bank-login-support.com Failure to verify your account within 24 hours will result in permanent suspension. Thank you, Bank Security Team 1. Sender Information - Email Address: support@bank-verify.com - Suspicious Traits: Domain not linked to a real bank (spoofed domain). 2. Subject Line - Text: "Urgent Action Required - Account Suspended" - Phishing Trait: Creates urgency and fear to provoke quick action. 3. Suspicious Link - Link Text:http://secure-bank-login-support.com - Real Destination: Not a legitimate banking domain, uses HTTP (not secure). - Trait: Mismatched and unsafe URL. 4. Email Content Traits - Urgency:"verify your account within 24 hours" - Threats:"permanent suspension" - Language Pressuring and manipulative tone. 5. Grammar and Format - Generic greeting: “Dear Customer” - Inconsistent tone and minor grammar issues. 6. Attachments - (none in the sample), phishing emails often include malicious attachments to access the data. 7. Conclusion This email is a clear phishing attempt, with spoofed sender identity, a suspicious and unsafe link, and psychological tactics designed to panic the recipient into clicking a fraudulent link. Recommendation for people: Never click on suspicious links or share sensitive information via email. Report phishing attempts to your email provider or cybersecurity team.
